Research into **Dense Plasma Focus (DPF)** plasmoid formation, particularly at the **PF-1000** facility, has moved toward a "self-organization" paradigm that links theoretical **Turner Relaxed States** to experimental **Poloidal Magnetic Flux Emission (PMFE)** and high fusion yields.

### 1. Turner Relaxed States & Force-Free Configurations
The **Turner Relaxed State (TRS)**, based on Leaf Turner’s 1986 theory, describes the minimum energy state of a **Hall Magnetohydrodynamic (HMHD)** fluid. 
*   **Beyond Taylor States:** Unlike Taylor relaxation (purely force-free, $j = \alpha B$), TRS accounts for the **Hall effect** and allows for **finite plasma pressure** and **spontaneous rotation** (mass flow).
*   **Physical Significance:** It explains the spontaneous generation of axial/poloidal magnetic fields ($B_z$) and azimuthal currents in a system that nominally begins with only axial current ($j_z$) and azimuthal field ($B_\theta$). The result is a **force-free helical configuration** within the plasmoid that can trap ions.

### 2. Poloidal Magnetic Flux Emission (PMFE)
**PMFE** is the experimental signature of the Turner Relaxed State. 
*   **Definition:** It is the detection of "returning" poloidal magnetic flux outside the DPF electrodes. In a simple Z-pinch, no such flux should exist.
*   **Correlation:** Research by **S.K.H. Auluck** and **M. Upadhyay** (2023) at PF-1000 has shown that PMFE signals correlate temporally with neutron emission. High-yield shots exhibit clear, reproducible PMFE signatures, indicating the formation of robust, self-organized magnetic "bottles" or trapping structures.

### 3. Evidence from PF-1000 (Kubes et al. 2016, 2024, 2025)
Pavel Kubes' group has provided extensive experimental evidence for the "plasmoid-as-fusion-engine" model:
*   **Plasmoid Characteristics:** High-yield shots ($Y_n \approx 10^{11}–10^{12}$) are characterized by **larger plasmoid volumes** (up to 4 cm³), **lower densities** (~2 × 10¹⁸ cm⁻³), and higher **stored magnetic energy**.
*   **Filamentary Structures:** 2024/2025 studies using high-resolution interferometry resolved sub-millimeter filaments within these plasmoids. These filaments carry poloidal current and undergo **magnetic reconnection**, which is the primary driver for particle acceleration.
*   **The Reconnection Engine:** Fusion occurs primarily via a **beam-target mechanism** triggered by the decay of these self-organized structures. The reconnection induces strong electric fields that accelerate deuterons to hundreds of keV.

### 4. Yield Correlations & Scaling
Kubes argues that the traditional **$I^4$ scaling law** fails at MA-scale currents because the yield becomes dependent on **plasmoid volume and energy density** rather than just peak current.
*   **Energy Scaling:** Neutron yield scales as $Y_n \propto W_m$, where $W_m$ is the magnetic energy stored in the plasmoid.
*   **Volume Factor:** A larger plasmoid volume provides a larger "reaction zone" and higher total inductance ($L$), allowing for more efficient energy conversion into fast ions. If the plasmoid volume "saturates" or fragments poorly, the yield plateaus regardless of increasing current.

### 5. Simulation Approach: Hybrid MHD-PIC
Capturing the self-organization of plasmoids requires a multi-scale approach:
*   **MHD (Fluid):** Captures the large-scale sheath transport, "necking" (m=0 instabilities), and the fluid-based self-organization into toroidal structures.
*   **PIC (Kinetic):** Essential for modeling the **magnetic reconnection** and the resulting **ion acceleration**. Hybrid codes (e.g., **Shmelev’s Hybrid MHD-PIC**) treat electrons as a fluid but ions as macroparticles, allowing the simulation to resolve the non-thermal "tails" of the ion distribution responsible for the neutron yield.
